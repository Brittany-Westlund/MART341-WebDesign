How to Create a Succulent Candle in a Glass Jar

As the air turns crisp and the leaves change to vibrant shades of orange and red, autumn brings a sense of warmth and coziness to our lives. It’s a time for savoring hot drinks, bundling up in soft layers, and enjoying the simple pleasures of the season. From pumpkin patches to scenic hikes through colorful forests, autumn invites us to slow down and appreciate the beauty around us. Whether you’re curled up with a good book or exploring the great outdoors, fall is a season full of charm and nostalgia.

Materials

  • Glass jar (clear)
  • Candle wax (soy, paraffin, or beeswax)
  • Wick (cotton or wood)
  • Wick sticker and metal holder
  • Mold for succulents (silicone)
  • Essential oils or fragrance (optional)
  • Double boiler or microwave-safe container
  • Thermometer
  • Stirring stick
  • Scissors
  • Color dye (optional)
  • Safety stickers

Instructions

1

Prepare Your Workspace

Clear a flat surface and gather your materials. Lay down newspaper or a drop cloth to protect your workspace from wax spills.

2

Choose Your Wick

Decide between a cotton wick or a wood wick. Cotton wicks are easy to work with while wood wicks create a cozy crackling sound.

3

Melt the Wax

In a double boiler, heat wax over water on medium. In a microwave, heat wax in a safe container in 30-second intervals.

4

Add Fragrance and Color

Add essential oils and color dye to the melted wax. Stir well to combine. Follow the recommended usage rate for your chosen wax type.

5

Secure the Wick

Attach base of wick to the center of jar using a sticker and metal holder. This helps keep the wick in place when pouring the wax.

6

Prepare the Succulents

Use a silicone mold to create a variety of wax succulents. Fill the succulent molds with melted wax and let set for at least 30 min.

7

Pour the Wax

Once wax reaches desired temperature, slowly pour into glass jar. Leave space at the top for the succulents. Ensure the wick stays centered.

8

Add the Succulents

Allow the wax to cool slightly but not fully harden (about 140°F or 60°C). Then, carefully set the succulents into the wax.

9

Finish Pouring

If needed, you can pour a little more wax to cover the bases of the succulents to secure them in place. Ensure the wick remains centered.

10

Let It Set

Let the candle cool and harden fully at room temperature, which may take several hours. Avoid moving the candle for an even surface.

11

Trim the Wick

After the candle has fully set, trim the wick to about 1/4 inch above the wax surface. This length promotes an even burn and reduces smoke.

12

Enjoy Your Candle

Light your new succulent candle and enjoy the ambiance it creates! Remember to always monitor the candle while it burns.
